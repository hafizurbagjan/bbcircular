The Banking Circular

Banking System in Bangladesh

Banking System in Bangladesh

The banking system in Bangladesh consists of a mix of public, private, and foreign banks, playing a critical role in the country’s economy. Here are some key features:

1. Types of Banks

  • Commercial Banks: These include both state-owned and private banks. State-owned banks are government-run, while private banks are operated by private organizations.
  • Islamic Banks: Bangladesh has a significant number of Islamic banks that operate under Sharia law, providing services without interest.
  • Development Banks: They focus on providing long-term financing for development projects, especially in sectors like agriculture and industry.

2. Regulatory Authority

The Bangladesh Bank (BB) is the central bank, responsible for regulating the country’s monetary and financial system, issuing currency, and managing foreign exchange.

3. Banking Regulations

The banking sector is regulated by the Bangladesh Bank, which sets policies regarding liquidity, capital, interest rates, and lending standards to ensure financial stability.

4. Technological Advancements

The banking sector has seen significant technological advancements, including online banking, mobile banking, and ATM services, improving accessibility for customers.

5. Microfinance

Bangladesh is known for its microfinance sector, promoting financial inclusion by providing small loans to the underprivileged and rural populations.

6. Challenges

The banking sector faces challenges such as high rates of non-performing loans, governance issues, and the need for better risk management practices.

7. Recent Developments

The sector has been evolving with new products, increased foreign investment, and the adoption of digital banking solutions to enhance customer service and operational efficiency.

General Banking

General Banking System in Bangladesh

The general banking system in Bangladesh refers to the core banking functions that commercial banks provide to individuals, businesses, and organizations. It includes services such as account opening, cash transactions, fund transfers, cheque clearing, remittances, and other financial services.

1. Account Services

  • Savings Account – For individual depositors with a limit on transactions.
  • Current Account – For businesses and frequent transactions.
  • Fixed Deposit Account (FDR) – Deposits for a fixed period at higher interest rates.
  • Recurring Deposit Account – Customers deposit a fixed amount every month for a specific term.

2. Cash Transactions

  • Cash Deposit
  • Cash Withdrawal
  • Cheque Transactions

3. Clearing and Collection of Cheques

Banks process cheques through the Bangladesh Automated Clearing House (BACH) under Bangladesh Bank.

4. Fund Transfers

  • BEFTN – Electronic transfer of funds between banks.
  • RTGS – Instant large-value transactions.
  • Online Banking & Mobile Banking – Internet-based fund transfers.

5. Remittance Services

  • Inward Remittance – Foreign currency sent to Bangladesh.
  • Outward Remittance – Sending money abroad.

6. ATM, Debit, and Credit Card Services

Banks provide ATMs, debit, and credit cards for convenient transactions.

7. Internet and Mobile Banking

Most banks now offer digital services for checking balances, paying bills, and transferring funds.

8. Loan and Advance Services

  • Personal Loans
  • Home Loans
  • Car Loans
  • Business Loans

9. Foreign Exchange and Trade Services

  • Letter of Credit (LC)
  • Foreign Currency Exchange
  • Export & Import Transactions

10. Compliance and Security Measures

  • Know Your Customer (KYC)
  • Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policies
  • Cybersecurity Measures

Regulation and Governance

The Bangladesh Bank regulates all commercial banks under the Banking Companies Act 1991.

Conclusion

The general banking system in Bangladesh plays a vital role in financial and economic development, ensuring smooth banking operations and digital transactions.

Credit Operations

Loan System in Bangladesh

The loan system in Bangladesh is regulated by the Bangladesh Bank, which oversees all financial institutions and ensures compliance with banking laws and policies. Banks and Non-Banking Financial Institutions (NBFIs) provide different types of loans to individuals, businesses, and organizations.

1. Types of Loans in Bangladesh

A. Personal Loans

  • Salary Loans
  • Home Loans
  • Car Loans
  • Education Loans
  • Medical Loans

B. Business Loans

  • Working Capital Loans
  • Term Loans
  • Trade Finance Loans
  • SME Loans
  • Women Entrepreneur Loans

C. Agricultural and Rural Loans

  • Crop Loans
  • Livestock & Fisheries Loans
  • Microfinance Loans

D. Industrial and Corporate Loans

  • Large Industrial Loans
  • Infrastructure Loans
  • Export Credit

2. Loan Process in Bangladesh

  • Application
  • Credit Assessment
  • Loan Approval
  • Disbursement
  • Repayment

3. Interest Rates and Repayment Terms

Interest rates are set by individual banks but regulated by Bangladesh Bank. Rates vary based on loan type, borrower profile, and economic conditions. Loan tenure can range from 6 months to 30 years, depending on the loan category.

4. Regulations and Policies

  • Banking Companies Act 1991
  • Microcredit Regulatory Authority (MRA)
  • Prudential Guidelines for Banks
  • Bangladesh Bank Risk-Based Capital Adequacy Framework

5. Loan Default and Recovery

  • Non-Performing Loans (NPLs)
  • Loan Rescheduling
  • Legal Action

Conclusion

The loan system in Bangladesh plays a critical role in economic growth by providing financial support to individuals and businesses. With strict regulations and various loan products, banks aim to promote responsible lending and financial inclusion.

Foreign Trade

Foreign Trade System in Bangladesh

The foreign trade system in Bangladesh is regulated by the Bangladesh Bank and the Ministry of Commerce. The country's trade policies aim to promote exports and regulate imports for economic growth.

1. Key Components of Foreign Trade

A. Export System

  • Readymade Garments (RMG) Industry
  • Agricultural and Jute Products
  • Leather and Footwear
  • Pharmaceuticals
  • Information Technology Services

B. Import System

  • Machinery and Equipment
  • Fuels and Petroleum Products
  • Raw Materials for Industries
  • Consumer Goods
  • Electronics and Vehicles

2. Trade Regulations and Policies

  • Import Policy Order and Export Policy Order
  • Bangladesh Tariff Structure
  • Foreign Exchange Regulations
  • Bangladesh Economic Zones Authority (BEZA) Policies
  • Special Incentives for Exporters

3. Trade Agreements and Partnerships

  • South Asian Free Trade Area (SAFTA)
  • Asia-Pacific Trade Agreement (APTA)
  • Generalized System of Preferences (GSP)
  • Bangladesh-European Union Trade Relations
  • Bangladesh-China and Bangladesh-India Trade Agreements

4. Challenges in Foreign Trade

  • Trade Deficit and Balance of Payments Issues
  • Infrastructure and Logistics Constraints
  • Customs and Bureaucratic Hurdles
  • Fluctuations in Exchange Rates
  • Global Market Competitiveness

Conclusion

The foreign trade system in Bangladesh is vital to its economic growth, with exports and imports playing a key role in industrial development. Continuous improvements in trade policies and global partnerships are essential for sustainable growth.

Branch Operations

Bank Branch Operations in Bangladesh

In Bangladesh, bank branch operations are regulated by the Bangladesh Bank (BB), the country's central bank. Banks operate under the Bank Companies Act 1991, which governs their functions, services, and compliance requirements.

1. Core Functions of a Bank Branch

a) Deposit Services

  • Savings Accounts
  • Current Accounts
  • Fixed Deposit Receipts (FDR)
  • Recurring Deposit Accounts
  • Special Deposit Schemes (Pension Scheme, Monthly Savings, etc.)

b) Loan and Credit Services

  • Personal Loans
  • Home Loans
  • SME (Small and Medium Enterprise) Loans
  • Agricultural Loans
  • Corporate and Industrial Loans
  • Car Loans
  • Credit Cards and Overdraft Facilities

c) Payment and Remittance Services

  • Electronic Fund Transfers (BEFTN, RTGS)
  • Mobile Financial Services (bKash, Nagad, Rocket, etc.)
  • Swift Transfers (International Remittance)
  • Payroll and Pension Disbursements
  • Bill Payments (Electricity, Water, Gas, etc.)

d) Trade and Foreign Exchange Services

  • Issuance of Letter of Credit (LC)
  • Import and Export Financing
  • Foreign Exchange Transactions
  • Handling Foreign Currency Accounts
  • Processing Student Files for Foreign Study Payments

e) Digital Banking Services

  • Online and Mobile Banking
  • ATM and POS Transactions
  • E-commerce Payments
  • Internet Banking Transfers

2. Regulatory and Compliance Requirements

  • Bangladesh Bank Guidelines: All banks must follow BB regulations.
  • Anti-Money Laundering (AML) & Know Your Customer (KYC): Banks must verify customer identity.
  • Interest Rate & Loan Classification: BB controls lending rates.
  • Corporate Governance: Banks must maintain transparency.

3. Challenges in Bank Branch Operations

  • Slow Digital Transformation
  • Cybersecurity Risks
  • Regulatory Compliance Updates
  • High Non-Performing Loans (NPLs)
  • Competition from Fintech & Mobile Banking

4. Future Trends in Branch Banking

  • Increased Automation
  • Agent Banking Expansion
  • Green Banking Initiatives
  • Blockchain & AI Adoption

Bangladesh Bank

Bangladesh Bank Operations

Bangladesh Bank (BB) is the central bank of Bangladesh, responsible for managing the country’s monetary policy and financial system. It was established on December 16, 1971, following the independence of Bangladesh.

Core Operations of Bangladesh Bank:

1. Monetary Policy Implementation:

  • Controlling inflation
  • Regulating interest rates
  • Managing the money supply

2. Currency Issuance:

  • Issuing and managing the Bangladeshi Taka (BDT)
  • Preventing counterfeit currency

3. Foreign Exchange Management:

  • Managing foreign reserves
  • Regulating foreign exchange markets
  • Controlling exchange rates and remittances

4. Supervision and Regulation of Banks:

  • Licensing commercial banks and financial institutions
  • Monitoring banking activities to ensure financial stability
  • Imposing regulations to prevent money laundering and terrorist financing

5. Credit Policy Management:

  • Providing loans to banks and financial institutions
  • Promoting SME and agricultural financing
  • Supporting green banking and sustainable finance

6. Maintaining Financial Stability:

  • Managing inflation and economic growth
  • Ensuring price stability
  • Controlling liquidity in the market

7. Payment Systems and Digital Transactions:

  • Overseeing the electronic payment systems
  • Encouraging digital banking and fintech innovation
  • Ensuring secure and efficient transactions

8. Foreign Reserve Management:

  • Managing the country's foreign currency reserves
  • Supporting international trade and investment

9. Promoting Financial Inclusion:

  • Supporting microfinance institutions
  • Encouraging mobile banking services
  • Ensuring access to banking services in rural areas

10. Government Debt Management:

  • Managing government bonds and securities
  • Handling public debt and deficit financing
